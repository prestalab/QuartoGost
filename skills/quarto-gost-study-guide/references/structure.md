## Baseline structure for a study guide

- титульный лист
- аннотация
- сведения о рекомендации, допуске, или утверждении, если они есть
- введение
- основные главы или модули
- примеры, задачи, упражнения, контрольные вопросы
- заключение
- список источников
- приложения

## Notes from GOST 7.60-2003

- `Учебное пособие` is a learning edition that supplements or partially or fully replaces a textbook.
- The status of the edition should be explicit in the front matter when approved as a study guide.
- Related subtypes include `учебно-методическое пособие`, `практикум`, and similar didactic formats.

## Notes from GOST R 7.0.4-2020

- Educational editions should carry structured output details on the title page and related front-matter elements.
- Typical title-page elements visible in the standard examples include:
  author or authors,
  title,
  edition type such as `учебное пособие`,
  recommendation or approval statement,
  edition number,
  place,
  publisher,
  year.

## Local author-original requirements

- File format: `.docx`
- Suggested filename pattern: `Фамилия – Название.docx`
- A4 portrait
- Margins: left 1.9 cm, right 1.9 cm, top 1.9 cm, bottom 2.4 cm
- Footer distance: 1.5 cm
- Font: Times New Roman
- Size: 16 pt
- Spacing: single
- Alignment: justified
- First-line indent: 1.0 to 1.5 cm
- Automatic hyphenation is expected
- Each chapter starts on a new page
- Subsections continue on the current page
- Each section should contain at least two subsections
- Each chapter should end with reinforcement material for students
- Heading level 1 uses uppercase letters
- Heading level 2 and deeper use lowercase letters
- Index numbering of headings is recommended
- Formula numbers are chapter-based, for example `3.2`
- Tables and illustrations are placed after the paragraph that references them

## Title and verso reminders

- The main title page should include organization, author, title, edition type, place, publisher, and year.
- The verso should include UDC, BBK, reviewers, bibliographic description, annotation, and publication notes.
- The cover is submitted separately as its own DOCX file.
