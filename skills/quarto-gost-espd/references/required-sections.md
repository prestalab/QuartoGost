## Notes

- The exact section set depends on the document kind inside the GOST 19 family.
- Use the ESPD template when title sheets, approval sheets, or change sheets
  must be preserved from the Word reference file.

## GOST 2.105-95 formatting and structure checkpoints

- Text documents may be predominantly continuous text or tabular/graph-based.
- Paragraph indent is typically 15-17 mm in the text document examples from the standard.
- If approval or agreement signatures are needed, use a title sheet and/or approval sheet.
- A change-registration sheet is recommended for text documents.
- Sections are numbered with Arabic numerals without a trailing dot.
- Subsections are numbered within sections: `1.1`, `1.2`, ...
- Points are numbered within a section or subsection depending on structure: `1.1`, `1.1.1`, ...
- Subpoints may be used when needed: `4.2.1.1`, `4.2.1.2`, ...
- Enumerations inside points use a dash, or a lowercase letter with a parenthesis when the text must refer to an item.
- A second-level enumeration may use Arabic numerals with a parenthesis.
- Large documents may be divided into parts and books; each part/book gets its own designation and naming.

## Title and approval sheet notes

- The title sheet may include product/document name, designation, approving and agreeing signatures, and related service fields.
- If an approval sheet exists, the title sheet should include the mark that the document is approved by that sheet.
- If many signatures are required, continuation sheets are allowed.
- Use the Word reference document to preserve these fields rather than rebuilding them manually in QMD.

## Shared custom styles

- Use the shared contract in `skills\quarto-gost-workflow\references\custom-styles.md`.
- In ESPD-like documents the most common custom styles are:
  `UnnumberedHeadingOne`, `UnnumberedHeadingOneNoTOC`, `AppendixHeadingOne`,
  `UnnumberedHeadingTwo`, `AbbreviationsTable`, and `ReferenceItem`.
