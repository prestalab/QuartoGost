## Norm-control checklist for dissertations and synopses

- Does the dissertation include all required structural elements for the selected format?
- Does the introduction include relevance, problem development level, goal, tasks, novelty, significance, methods, defense statements, reliability, and approbation?
- Does each chapter start on a new page?
- Are margins, line spacing, and font size aligned with the standard or local institutional rules?
- Is page numbering continuous, with the title page counted but not visibly numbered?
- Are headings centered and written without a final period?
- Are figures and tables placed near the first reference to them?
- Are defense statements concrete, result-oriented, and mirrored consistently in the synopsis?
- Does the synopsis cover include mandatory fields?
- Does the reverse side of the synopsis cover include organization, supervisors, opponents, leading organization, defense date/place, library, mailing date, and academic secretary?
